    • PROJECT VOW(Vocational Skills for Rural Women)

    Project Description

    HMI Project VOW(Vocational Skills for Rural Women) is training women on various vocational skills and gives them technical support in form of advising on best suppliers, best markets, suggesting innovative activities to avoid market saturation and linking beneficiaries to each other so that they can complement each others activities.


    This involves acquisition of both technical and vocational skills; having at the back of our mind that skills development is vital in providing job creation, promoting growth, reducing unemployment fighting inequality and poverty eradication. Employable skills are not only developed in schools, but also often in alternative programmes, such as vocational centers schools, or through work based training.


    Gaining practical training can be a means of securing employment and leading a productive life for women and adolescent girls who have missed out from basic education and formal schooling or who did not manage to gain skills for work through their education.This is a great deal prerequisite for the women and girls in the poorest rural communities.


    For the empowerment of women to have lasting impacts, HMI is igniting dialogs and raising consciousness so that the social construction of gender, which subordinates women in the family, class, religion or society, can be changed.

    Goal

    The goal of Project VOW(Vocational Skills for Rural Women) is to promote gender equality and empower women through suitable vocational skills program programmes.


    Objectives

    *To contribute to the improvement of women's employment rates by establishing centres specifically for women.


    *To promote the wellbeing of women and their children through vocational skills training by exploring themes that address their concerns.


    *To organize women's groups to encourage women to attend vocational centres more regularly and to understand the importance of participation in lifelong vocational centres.


    *To promote the learning of simple techniques and income-generating skills (sewing, knitting, market gardening and culinary arts) to allow women to carry out income-generating activities.


    *To grant micro-credits to women to support their practice of income-generating activities.


    Implementation Methodology and Strategy

    A preliminary analysis of beneficiaries needs is made taking into account their real-life situations. This analysis informs the design of vocational techniques around the beneficiary. This includes life skills relating to the economic and socio-cultural development of young and adult women of child-bearing age.


    The projects ensure that lectures are delivered in local languages in other to capture the attention of the learners most especially the rural women.

    Recruitment and training of facilitators

    Instructors are generally recruited as volunteers.


    Enrolment of learners

    As well as adult women, the programme also targets young women over the age of 15 and people from some ethnic groups.


    Sustainability of the Project

    Her Matter Initiative (HMI) will continue to provide assistance to the communities through project VOW. We are committed to the Susainable Development Goals (SDGs). This Vocational Skills for Rural Women is a small significant glimpse into some of the women upliftment activities that we undertake. Funding is therefore needed in order to best realize the goal of succeeding in this initiative. It is on this note that we are calling upon funding organizations, partners and generous individuals to give a helping hand towards the realization and sustainability of this project. Through this, the communities are provided with helpful interventions and hopeful information and assistance.
