Impacts of Project PROG
(Promotion of Girls' Education)

Sherifah, student, age 8

When HMI came to our school to share writing materials, school bags and thought us how to use the computer, I was very happy because it is an assurance that I will remain in school.


Kudu, age 6

I have never been to school before because my parent think its not necessary, but I am very happy that HMI was able to convince my parents to enroll me in school. Now I can go to school like my friends and we can read and write together. I want to be a teacher when I grow up so that I can teach children ABC and 123 so that they can be happy as I am happy now.


Celilia, Student, age 7

I have developed basic educational skill and computer literacy after HMI came to our village with teachers and computer instructors. I have gained confidence in using the computer; interact with English language to some extent and now I have better plans for my future.




HMI project PROG (Promotion of Girl Child Education) has impacted the lives of over 500 girls and 300 women. HMI shall always be in the vanguard in promoting the girl child education and accessible community learning. With such opportunities girls will remain in school and women who became interested and exited during their first learning process will want more opportunities.

Her Matter Initiative (HMI)

HMI is investing qualitative resources into ensuring that Nigerian women and girls are empowered towards the actualization of the gender angle the UN Sustainable Development Goals (SDGs).
